About Me

Pioneering research in battery materials with expertise in cathodes, anodes, and separators for next-generation energy storage systems.

As a dedicated battery researcher with over 6 years of specialized expertise, I have pioneered breakthrough innovations in electrochemical energy storage. My work includes developing Prussian blue cathodes with exceptional stability (>76% retention over 1000 cycles), creating nitrogen-doped carbon anodes with triple the capacity of traditional graphite, and engineering advanced PVDF-HFP composite separators.

I've had the privilege of collaborating with Nobel laureate Prof. John B. Goodenough and have contributed to the scientific community through 21 SCI-indexed publications and 2 patents. Currently, I'm advancing sustainable energy solutions at the Yangtze Delta Region Institute, UESTC.
